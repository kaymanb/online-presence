I am currently an MSc student in the Theory Group at the University of Toronto, where I am fortunate to be supervised by Prof. Faith Ellen. My research interests are in computability, logic, complexity theory, and the theory of distributed computing.

Before that, I completed my undergraduate degree in computer science and mathematics, also at the University of Toronto. As an undergrad I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Peter Marbach on a research project in mathematical models for social networks. I also worked as a software engineer at Flipp and RBC Capital Markets.

I am passionate about hiking, canoeing, cycling, and anything related to the great outdoors. You can find photos from some of my trips here. I am also a big fan of literary fiction, and I keep an infrequently-updated list of books I've enjoyed here.
